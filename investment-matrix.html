The Framework

5 Categories Every Home Falls Into

I've watched homeowners flush thousands of dollars into the wrong upgrades. The Investment Matrix tells you which of these five categories your home is in — so you spend where it counts and skip what doesn't.

Category 1
81–100 pts

Restoration Required

Aging systems, significant structural issues. Focus only on essential repairs and disclosure items. Your buyer is likely an investor — don't overspend on cosmetics.

Category 2
61–80 pts

Concealable Condition

Outdated mechanicals with noticeable wear. Deep clean, minor repairs, and touch-ups only. Don't sink money into big updates that won't pay off.

Category 3
41–60 pts

Surface Shine

Looks great on the surface but savvy buyers will scrutinize the fundamentals. Address deferred maintenance and stage well — but don't over-invest in trends.

Category 4
31–40 pts

Unpolished Potential

Well-maintained mechanicals, dated aesthetics. My favorite category — small updates yield big returns. Kitchen/bath refreshes and curb appeal will pay off here.

Category 5
20–30 pts

Modern Move-In

Updated, turnkey, and ready. Focus on maintenance, minor touch-ups, and killer listing photos. Buyers expect perfection — don't let deferred maintenance derail you.
